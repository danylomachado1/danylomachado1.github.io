About

About Me

I'm a Physics PhD student at Illinois Institute of Technology, where I work on neutrino physics and nuclear nonproliferation. My research uses neutrino signals to monitor nuclear activity, combining experimental physics with computational modeling.

Previously, I've worked at CERN as part of the LHCb Computational Group, conducted research at the University of Alberta on dark matter detection, and contributed to cosmic-ray detector calibration in Peru.

I'm passionate about bridging fundamental physics with real-world applications in science and technology policy.

Research

Research Experience

Neutrino Physics Jul – Dec 2023

MAD(ish) Test Site Transparency Study

Advisor: Dr. Bryce Littlejohn — IIT, Illinois

Determined experiment parameters for using neutrinos to set limits on fission-derived yield in large explosions. Predicted flux and identified optimal detector configurations using the PROSPECT-CNP toolkit.

Data Acquisition Jun – Sep 2021

Data Transfer: LHCb DAQ & FPGA Accelerators

Advisor: Dr. Riccardo Fantechi — CERN, Switzerland

Reviewed the LHCb data acquisition system and tested data transfer between PCIe40, EB, and the RETINA buffer. Developed efficient methods for merging data streams using firmware characteristics.

Detector Calibration Oct 2020 – Mar 2021

Water Cherenkov Detector Calibration

Advisors: Dr. Cesar Castromonte, Luis Otiniano — UNI, Peru

Analyzed experimental data for vertical muons collected in Buenos Aires. Proposed a simulation for PMT response using CORSIKA and GEANT4.

Dark Matter Jan – Mar 2020

A View through the PICO 40L Cameras

Advisors: Dr. Carsten Krauss, Scott Fallows — U of Alberta, Canada

Studied camera behavior in the PICO 40L dark matter detector to improve trigger efficiency, monitor stability, and identify phenomena for future analysis.
