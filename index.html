Franz Machado

Physics PhD Student

Illinois Institute of Technology

I'm a physicist working at the intersection of neutrino physics and nuclear nonproliferation. My research uses neutrino signals to monitor nuclear activity, combining experimental detector physics with computational modeling via the PROSPECT-CNP toolkit.

Previously, I worked at CERN on the LHCb data acquisition system, conducted dark matter detector research at the University of Alberta, and contributed to cosmic-ray detector calibration at Universidad Nacional de Ingeniería in Peru.
